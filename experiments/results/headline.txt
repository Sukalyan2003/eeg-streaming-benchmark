RESULT 1 (zero-phase regime, win=8 s):
  boundary RMSE naive=14.930 uV -> overlap(0.5 s)=0.275 uV : reduction 34.7 dB at +-7% latency (0.633 -> 0.589 ms/window).

RESULT 2 (zero-phase length floor):
  An order-200 FIR (202 taps) has filtfilt padlen=606 samples; zero-phase filtering needs at least 607 samples (3.04 s). Shorter windows must use a causal filter instead.
  A windowed filter processes a span of chunk + 2*overlap. Large windows clear the floor and are zero-phase; small windows do not:
    e.g. chunk=1 s + overlap=0.5 s = 400 samples (<607) => filtfilt_active=0, interior RMSE=30.67 uV (distorted everywhere, not just seams).
  Design criterion to stay zero-phase: keep chunk + 2*overlap >= 3.04 s (>= 1.02 s overlap each side at a 1 s chunk), i.e. (chunk + 2*overlap)*fs > 3*taps.
