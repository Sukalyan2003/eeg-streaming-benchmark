Cascaded-tapering pitfall in relative band power (synthetic, 8 trials):

Welch (manual Hann + welch's per-segment Hann = double taper):
  max per-band discrepancy vs MNE psd_array_welch:
    double taper (manual Hann + welch) = 11.90 percentage points
    single taper (welch only)          = 0.64 percentage points
  Pearson r (single-taper vs MNE, all bands x trials) = 0.99997.
  Per-band relative power (double / single / MNE):
     delta: double  22.94%  single  22.64%  mne  22.81%  (double vs mne 9.78 pp)
     theta: double   9.93%  single   9.42%  mne   9.28%  (double vs mne 3.07 pp)
     alpha: double  60.95%  single  62.10%  mne  62.20%  (double vs mne 11.90 pp)
      beta: double   5.48%  single   5.18%  mne   5.05%  (double vs mne 1.01 pp)
     gamma: double   0.70%  single   0.66%  mne   0.66%  (double vs mne 0.16 pp)

Multitaper (manual Hann before DPSS tapers vs plain multitaper reference):
  with pre-Hann    : max discrepancy 9.08 pp (pre-windowing corrupts it too)
  without pre-Hann : max discrepancy 0.00 pp (matches the reference)

Takeaway: taper exactly once. Pre-windowing before an estimator that already tapers
biases relative band power, most on the dominant band, for both Welch and multitaper.
