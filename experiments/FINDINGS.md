Metrics and figures in `results/`.

## Setup
- 120 s, 4-channel synthetic EEG, 200 Hz, with band oscillations, 1/f noise, and 50 Hz mains.
- Filters: the production `domain.helpers.filters.create_bandpass_filter` (FIR order 200,
  0.5–30 Hz) + `create_notch_filter` (50 Hz). Ground truth = whole-signal filtering.
- Strategies: naive per-window vs overlap-add (extended window, keep center) — the production
  fix from `file-service/changelogs/OVERLAP_ADD_FIX_APPLIED.md`.

## Result 1 — Overlap-add eliminates seam artifacts (zero-phase regime, 8 s windows)
- Boundary RMSE vs ground truth: **naive 14.93 µV → overlap-add (0.5 s) 0.275 µV = 34.7 dB
  reduction**; overlap-add (1.0 s) → 3.3e-4 µV (≈ eliminated).
- Latency cost is **~1%** (0.556 → 0.564 ms/window): the improvement is effectively free.
- Naive filtering concentrates error at seams (boundary-to-interior ratio +60 dB);
  overlap-add removes it (`fig_seam.png`, `fig_profile.png`, `fig_summary.png`).

## Result 2 — The `filtfilt` length floor (a regime effect, conditional production concern)
- `create_bandpass_filter` uses zero-phase `filtfilt` only when the input has
  **≥ 3 × len(taps) = 606 samples (3.03 s @ 200 Hz)**; shorter inputs **silently** fall back to
  a phase-distorting causal `lfilter` path (`domain/domain/helpers/filters.py:229-238`).
- The streaming path filters a window of `db_chunk_size + 2 × overlap`. The **default**
  `db_chunk_size = 10 s` (`file-service/src/routes/data_streaming.py:367-370`) with
  `_FILTER_OVERLAP_S = 0.5 s` (`.../streaming/helpers.py:69`) → 11 s = 2200 samples → **filtfilt
  runs; the default is zero-phase and correct.**
- **The concern is the configurable small-chunk regime.** `chunk_size` is clamped only to
  `[0.5, 60] s`, so a client requesting a small chunk silently enters the fallback regime:
  chunk = 1 s + overlap 0.5 s = **400 samples (< 606)** → `filtfilt_active = 0`, interior RMSE
  30.7 µV (distorted everywhere, not just seams). To stay zero-phase at a 1 s chunk, overlap
  must be **≥ 1.01 s each side**, or keep `chunk + 2 × overlap ≥ 3.03 s`. The `metrics.csv`
  `filtfilt_active` column marks the regime boundary.

## So what (paper framing)
The contribution is not merely "overlap-add fixes seams." It is a characterization of the
interaction between **FIR order**, **`filtfilt`'s 3×(order+1) length floor**, **display window
size**, and **overlap**, which jointly determine (a) whether streaming EEG filtering is
zero-phase at all and (b) whether seam transients are suppressed. We quantify both regimes and
derive the minimum context, with a concrete recommendation for production parameters.

## Product note (for the engineering backlog)
The default streaming chunk (10 s + 0.5 s overlap) reaches `filtfilt` and is zero-phase. The
risk is the configurable small-chunk regime: `chunk_size` is clamped only to `[0.5, 60] s`, so
a chunk < ~2 s drops below the floor and silently uses the distorting causal path. Guarantee
`(chunk + 2·overlap)·fs ≥ 3·len(taps)` at the streaming layer (raise the chunk floor, auto-grow
overlap for small chunks, or pad-to-floor-then-trim), and make the fallback observable.

## Result 3 — Filter magnitude fidelity and event mis-timing (`exp_filter_fidelity.py`)
- **Magnitude (zero-phase path):** passband (1–25 Hz) gain −0.64 ± 1.54 dB (ideal 0 dB),
  50 Hz notch −120 dB, 60 Hz stopband −120 dB → the intended response is realized
  (`fig_magnitude.png`).
- **Temporal shift of a 20 ms transient (proxy for an epileptiform spike):**
  - zero-phase (`filtfilt`) path: **0 ms** (no shift).
  - short-window fallback (`lfilter`) path: **+500 ms** — the linear-phase FIR group delay
    (~(taps−1)/2 ≈ 502 ms) is **not compensated** (`fig_transient_shift.png`).
- Combined with Result 2: whenever the streaming filter enters the fallback regime (small
  `chunk_size`), streamed/filtered EEG is **temporally shifted by ~0.5 s** — events appear half
  a second from their true time. At the default 10 s chunk this does not occur.

## Result 4 — Channel canonicalization and feature integrity (`exp_channels_features.py`)
- **Canonicalization:** family-classification accuracy **89.5%** on 19 real-world channel-name
  variants. Of the two disagreements: `EEG A1-REF` → `ref_site` is actually correct (ear/mastoid
  reference, not scalp EEG); `POL Fp1` → `aux` is a **genuine gap** — the Nihon-Kohden `POL`
  prefix is not stripped, so such EEG channels are misclassified and would be wrongly excluded
  from EEG montages.
- **Feature integrity:** computing an average-reference montage **without** excluding a
  mislabeled ECG channel inflates C3 alpha bandpower by **+48.7%** (58.68 vs 39.45). Correct
  quantitative features require channel-aware classification before montage.

## Result 5 — Bandpower equivalence vs MNE, and a double-Hann taper bug (`exp_bandpower_equiv.py`)
- Drives the **production** PSD routine
  (`workers/src/utils/extraction/band_power_optimized._compute_psd`, Welch nperseg=256/
  noverlap=128) and compares per-band **relative** power against MNE `psd_array_welch`.
- The methods are strongly correlated (Pearson r = **0.991**), but the production estimator
  differs from MNE by up to **11.9 percentage points** on the dominant band (alpha).
- **Root cause (verified):** `_compute_psd` applies `np.hanning()` to the whole window **and**
  `scipy.welch` applies its default per-segment Hann — a **double taper**. Disabling the
  redundant manual Hann drops the max discrepancy from **11.9 pp → 0.64 pp**, at which point
  the production estimator matches MNE.
- **Fix:** remove the manual Hann (let `welch` taper per segment), or pass `window='boxcar'`
  to `welch`. Until then, relative bandpower (a stored clinical feature) is biased.
- **Estimator-agnostic:** the same manual taper also corrupts the production **multitaper**
  path (which has its own DPSS tapers): max discrepancy vs MNE multitaper **9.1 pp** with the
  manual Hann, **0.0 pp** without — so the bug is in the shared pre-step, not the estimator.

## Result 6 — Real-data confirmation on CHB-MIT (`exp_realdata.py`)
External validity on one open-access clinical recording (PhysioNet CHB-MIT `chb01_01.edf`,
256 Hz, bipolar montage; raw EDF gitignored under `data/`):
- **R1 confirmed:** overlap-add reduces window-seam boundary RMSE from **12.96 µV → 0.049 µV
  (48.5 dB)** on real EEG (interior RMSE ≈ 0 → zero-phase interior). The effect is *larger* than
  on synthetic data.
- **R2 confirmed:** the `filtfilt` floor is `3 × len(taps) = 606 samples`, i.e. **2.37 s at
  256 Hz** (vs 3.03 s at 200 Hz) — the floor is in samples, so its duration scales with `fs`.
- **R3 confirmed:** the fallback group-delay shift measured by cross-correlation against the
  zero-phase output is **+391 ms**, matching the theoretical `(taps−1)/2/fs = 393 ms`. The shift
  is **fs-dependent** (~393 ms at 256 Hz vs ~500 ms at 200 Hz) — an honest nuance for the paper.
- Figure: `results/fig_realdata_seam.png`.

## Result 7 — Generalization across FIR order, the transition, and seed robustness (`exp_regime.py`)
- **FIR-order sweep:** the zero-phase floor is exactly `3 × (order+1)` taps and the fallback
  shift is `(taps−1)/2/fs`, both scaling with order: order 100 → floor 306 samples (1.53 s @
  200 Hz), shift 255 ms; 200 → 606 (3.03 s), 505 ms; 300 → 906 (4.53 s), 755 ms; 400 → 1206
  (6.03 s), 1005 ms. This generalizes the design criterion **`chunk + 2·overlap ≥ 3·taps`** to
  any FIR order (`regime_order_sweep.csv`, `fig_order_floor.png`).
- **Transition:** sweeping the total filtered length through the floor shows boundary error
  collapse sharply at `L = 3·taps` (`regime_transition.csv`, `fig_transition.png`).
- **Seed robustness (12 seeds, 8 s windows):** naive boundary RMSE **15.9 ± 4.1 µV**, overlap-add
  **0.273 ± 0.035 µV**, reduction **35.1 ± 2.3 dB** — the headline effect is stable, not a single
  draw.

## Result 8 — Real-data confirmation of R4–R5 on Siena (`exp_realdata_features.py`)
External validity on a recording with **genuine** non-EEG channels (PhysioNet Siena Scalp EEG
`PN00-1.edf`, 512 Hz, 10–20 monopolar EEG + a real `EKG EKG` channel + SpO2/HR; gitignored):
- **R4 confirmed:** canonicalization separates **all 29 EEG electrodes** (`EEG Fp1`→FP1, …) from
  the 6 genuine non-EEG channels (`EKG EKG`→`ecg`, `SPO2`/`HR`/markers→`aux`/`unknown`).
- **R5 confirmed:** including the **real ECG** channel in the average reference biases EEG C3
  relative alpha power by **+127.9%** (3.10% → 7.08%) — larger than the synthetic case, confirming
  channel-aware exclusion is required on real data. (Magnitude is recording-dependent; the
  direction/mechanism is robust.)
